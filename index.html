  • We Will Be Martians

    We Will Be Martians from Auden Rae on Vimeo.

    The brief for this extract of music was to create an original soundtrack for the provided film ‘We Will be Martians’, which is part of the Music Tech A Level exam. This was my first time attempting to create a 3 minute soundtrack for a space-related film.

    Upon initially watching the film, my first thoughts were that I wanted to create a story through my sound. As the film could be interpreted as a series of ‘outer space’ scenes with no correlation, my aim was to link the images with music and create a narrative. Furthermore, I set out to match most of the moving images with sounds that would be associated with the image e.g. the loud rumbling of a rocket ship. However, I didn’t want to give sound to each and every movement in the film, as that would make the piece become predictable. This decision was also made so as to leave the viewer with the freedom to imagine what sounds could have been used in the ‘empty moments’, giving the piece more interest and room for thought and discussion by the audience.

    I put together my story through my sound by creating motifs for specific scenes and images that reoccur in the film, for example, the use of the triangle when the flower seeds enter the frame. The use of the 4 note pattern was a strong highlight of this, as it reappears towards the end of the extract but is played through the strings instead of the synths heard at the start.

    As this piece is still a work in progress, I would like to adjust the levels of all the tracks further to reduce the distortion of the sound. Additionally, to fit the A Level brief, I will adjust some of the Logic stock synths and change the tracks to original synths I have created, and use some samples in the piece too - both original and others already created.
